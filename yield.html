The Yield Engine

Yield raises what every fan spends.

The 2026 agents proved the pattern inside the ticketing system of record. Yield is that same intelligence given the whole data platform: it runs the hunt across every application, on every client, against one governed metric truth, and it counts only the revenue that would not have arrived on its own. All data on this page is illustrative.

The Raw Material

The data platform hands Yield a finished fan record.

Every application writes to the same canonical fan, so the mart row already holds the whole wallet: ticket spend, merch spend, the attach flag, and a score for the next purchase. Nobody assembles a spreadsheet before the hunt begins. The record arrives finished.

The Mechanism

One query finds the money.

SELECT COUNT(*) FROM club_a.fan_360
WHERE plan_type = 'SEASON' AND merch_attach = FALSE;
4,212

season holders with zero merch history: a target list an agent can generate inside the system of record

The list flows into Fan Journey as a campaign, the offer ships under the club’s own brand, and the fans who respond post new transactions back onto the same data platform that found them. The loop closes in the data, and that closed loop is what makes the next section possible.

The Adjudication

The models predict, and the holdout decides.

Two models score every candidate. Propensity finds the fans who are likely to buy anyway. Uplift finds the fans who move only if asked. Yield spends its offers on the second group, because the first group was already coming.

Every campaign backtests against history before it ships. Every campaign keeps a holdout that never sees the offer. The difference between the offered group and the holdout is the lift, and only that lift posts to IFR/C. The number is earned before it is reported.

The Extension

Every application becomes a signal and a surface.

On the data platform, the intelligence that hunts in ticketing reads merch, concessions, the app, loyalty, and ingress as one record, and it can act through any of them: a concession credit for the fan who leaves at halftime, a renewal nudge timed to app engagement, a suite offer for the group organizer. Each application contributes signal, each application carries offers, and every measured dollar of lift lands in the same numerator: incremental fan revenue per client.
