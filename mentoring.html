Mentoring

The platform is a team before it is a system.

The Engineering page showed the code and the guides a team runs on. This page shows the method that produces those guides and develops the engineers who use them. Jump wrote people development into this role, and this page treats it the way the rest of the site treats architecture: as a discipline with a method, evidence, and a feedback loop.

The Method

Engineers learn to recognize patterns first.

DIGEST stands for Decomposed Integration for Genuine Skill Transfer. It is the method behind every guide on the Engineering page, compiled over years of mentoring technical and business teams. The core rule is ordering: material is decomposed so that no piece references anything that has not been taught yet, and each piece moves an engineer from recognizing a pattern to applying it to explaining it fluently.

The Lineage

The method extends a published discipline.

BEAM, from Corr and Stagnitto’s Agile Data Warehouse Design, taught that data models are gathered collaboratively and by example, around the business events people can actually describe. DIGEST applies the same instinct one layer up, to skill transfer: teach by worked example on real material, in the order a learner can absorb it.

The Method, Applied

The Engineering guides are the method at work.

The guides stay on the Engineering page, where the code lives. Each one takes a piece of Jump’s own stack and walks it in the delivery format the method prescribes: annotated code with its grain assumptions wired to tests, inline warnings that carry their provenance, and validation written as a query with its expected result.

The Audit Loop

Review is what makes the guides version.

Shipped work gets audited, and the findings feed back into the guides. The storage guide on the Engineering page carries a v2 status line because a v1 audit flagged a specific line, and the warning that replaced it names its origin. An engineer’s growth shows up the same way, as the measurable distance between their v1 and their v2.
