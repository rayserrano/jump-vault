The Premise

Nine products earn the revenue. One layer decides whether it compounds.

Jump’s products already earn the money: ticketing, commerce, pricing, the team apps, and the agents launched in 2026. Vault claims none of that revenue. It is the platform under the products, the fan data foundation that decides whether each one runs on every client at full speed. The yardstick is incremental: IFR/C, incremental fan revenue per client, revenue the products would not have earned on their own. The mechanism is compounding: every transaction enriches the fan record, a richer record raises the hit rate of the next offer, and this year’s gains join next year’s baseline. The rest of this page lays that out as three engines around one number.

The Three Engines

Every engine feeds a revenue line

VAULT YIELD

Raise spend per fan, on every client

AI agents run on a governed semantic layer and hunt revenue in fan data: the season holder who never bought merch, the merch buyer who never upgraded, the group-buy organizer who should be seeing suite offers. The same agents run bundling analysis across ticket, merch, and concession, and they find segments where engagement outruns monetization.

The mechanics are one governed metric truth per client mart and agents that query the system of record. Every incremental fan dollar drips a share into Jump’s line. The 2026 agent launch proved the pattern once. Yield repeats it on every client.

VAULT ONBOARD

Turn client acquisition into manufacturing

Today every new club is a bespoke integration, so onboarding capacity throttles the growth story. The fix is an integration layer on a canonical model. Under that layer, onboarding is a mapping exercise: ticketing, commerce, app, and historical CRM map into one universal model, and the client mart is stamped out on top.

The product metric is time-to-live-revenue per client. With multiple go-lives stacking in 2027, this engine is the difference between a queue and a production line.

VAULT BENCH

Revenue that does not exist yet

The multi-client foundation enables offerings only a unified platform can make:

  • Benchmarking tiers: your merch attach rate against league peers
  • Sponsorship intelligence: fan data that proves sponsor ROI
  • The long-horizon option, governance first: the cross-property fan graph itself

The Flywheel

Three engines, one compounding loop

Yield lifts fan spend → the take grows → documented lift becomes the sales deck → pipeline converts faster → Onboard gets clients live in weeks → more clients mean more data → Bench benchmarks sharpen → which lifts Yield again. The platform sits at the center of all three loops.

The North Star
IFR/C: Incremental Fan Revenue per Client

How many incremental fan dollars did the platform generate, per client, this quarter? Yield raises it. Onboard multiplies how many clients count toward it. Bench adds margin to it. It is one number the board, the sales deck, and the roadmap can all share, and it anchors the roadmap against feature creep: if an item does not move IFR/C, it waits.
