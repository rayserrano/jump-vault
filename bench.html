The Bench Engine

Bench creates revenue that does not exist yet.

Yield lifts the revenue lines a club already has, and Onboard brings new clubs onto them faster. Bench is a third kind of money: products only a multi-client foundation can offer, because their raw material is the view across properties. Governance is what makes that view sellable. All data on this page is illustrative.

The Benchmark

Every club wants to know where it stands.

A club’s merch attach rate means little on its own. Against league peers it becomes a roadmap. Bench publishes governed, anonymized benchmarks that every club feeds and every club receives: attach rates, renewal rates, and spend per fan by tier. Only a platform holding many properties on one metric truth can compute these honestly, because a benchmark is only as trustworthy as the shared definitions underneath it.

The Proposal

Sponsorship reporting is a product Jump could sell next.

Clubs sell sponsorships on attendance and impressions today. The data platform can prove more: how many scanned fans saw an activation, how many redeemed the offer, and how many came back afterward. Fan data that proves sponsor return raises the price of the inventory it measures. This appears nowhere in the nine shipped modules, and that is exactly why it belongs on this page: it is proposed, not built, and the data platform is what makes it buildable.

The Long Horizon

The fan graph stays an option until governance opens it.

The founding decision on the Onboard page already permits one person with edges to many clubs. Whether to ever activate cross-property products is a decision for clients, leagues, and lawyers, in that order. Bench treats the fan graph as an option the architecture preserves and governance prices. No product ships until the governance does, and the architecture’s job is to make sure that day arrives with the data already in order.
